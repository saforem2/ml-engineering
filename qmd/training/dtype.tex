\subsection{General OPs}\label{general-ops}

\texttt{LayerNorm}-like operations must not do their work in
half-precision, or they may lose a lot of data. Therefore when these
operations are implemented correctly they do efficient internal work in
fp32 and then their outputs are downcast to half-precision. Very often
it's just the accumulation that is done in fp32, since adding up
half-precision numbers is very lossy.

example:

\subsection{Reduction collectives}\label{reduction-collectives}

fp16: ok to do in fp16 if loss scaling is in place

bf16: only ok in fp32

\subsection{Gradient accumulation}\label{gradient-accumulation}

best done in fp32 for both, but definitely for bf16
